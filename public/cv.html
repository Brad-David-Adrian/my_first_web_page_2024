My C.V


The Computer Engineering curriculum combines principles of computer science and electrical engineering to prepare students for a versatile career.

The program begins with foundational courses in programming, mathematics, and physics, covering topics such as calculus, discrete mathematics, and digital logic design.

Core courses include data structures and algorithms, computer organization and architecture, embedded systems, and operating systems.

Students gain hands-on experience in microprocessor programming, circuit analysis, and software development.

Advanced topics like machine learning, computer networks, and cybersecurity are introduced alongside electives such as robotics, IoT, or VLSI design.

Practical skills are reinforced through labs, internships, and team-based projects like building hardware prototypes or developing software solutions.

In their final year, students undertake a capstone project that integrates hardware and software to solve real-world problems.

This comprehensive curriculum equips graduates for careers in hardware design, embedded systems, software development, or further specialization in graduate studies.
